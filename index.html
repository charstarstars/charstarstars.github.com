Here are some projects I've worked on

  • Color Me Timbers

    I worked with Michelle Lew, Michael Helmbrecht, and Parker Bossier during the Fall 2012 HackNY Hackathon.

    The concept behind the app was to be able to take a picture from your iOS device of what you're wearing, share it through foursquare, select color from the picture and be able to purchase color-matched items on Etsy.

  • MeterMaid

    One of the pains of driving in urban areas is finding parking. MeterMaid is an idea that attempts to alleviate that problem.

    This project was a user experience design exploration from start to finish. We went from contextual inquiry to paper prototypes to lo-fi wireframes to hi-fi interactive prototypes. By the end of the semester we had a interactive mocked-up front-end prototype.

    I worked with Ben Lanier and Sam Lavery on MeterMaid for the Designing Human-Centered Software course.

  • Tweeting Clock

    Built using Processing, the tweeting clock grabs tweets from twitter at every second and displays the username of that tweet's author. Every hour it grabs the top trending topic and displays it in the center. At the top of the minute the full tweet is displayed around the clock. The entire clock is colored by time.

    Download the Mac application

  • Video Jockey Now

    Built with Max/MSP for the final project in Prototyping User Interfaces class. The software takes audio input and lets the user choose a video and a visual effect and a frequency. The amplitude of the frequency determines a parameter of the visual effect. In the mock-up screenshot here, the effect being controlled is the blur.

  • Hackstic Surgery

    I teamed up with Bryan Wade and Max Hawkins for TartanHacks 2012, a 24-hour hackathon. We did a Kinect hack using your hands to manipulate an image and branding it as a "try-it before you buy it" approach to plastic surgery. We snagged second place overall.
